l.Introduction


Background and Significance of the Study

- Composting is very important to communities and the environment because this helps in reducing, reusing, and recycling one’s waste, which can also contribute to reducing pollution. According to UNEP (n.d.), composting aids in lowering greenhouse gas emissions that contribute to climate change by minimizing food waste. Approximately 8 to 10 percent of the world's greenhouse gas emissions are attributed to food loss and waste, and biodiversity is under increasing stress due to the increased usage of land and water resources. Secondly, according to Composting Council (n.d.), composting provides environmental advantages that are both immediate and indirect, and it also has the added advantage of preventing landfills from becoming overflowing with trash. This is because compost is created from waste materials like food scraps, organic leftovers, and other materials that would otherwise decay in landfills. Lastly, according to EPA 2020, composting reduces greenhouse gas emissions by increasing soil carbon sequestration and limiting methane emissions through aerobic decomposition, as methane-producing bacteria do not function in the presence of oxygen. This shows that composting is very important to communities and the environment because this helps in reducing, reusing, and recycling one’s waste, which can also contribute to reducing pollution.

- Our partner institution, which is the SAKADAB, can make use and benefit from composting in general, and from our group’s eggshell compost project by helping them reduce their waste (from their bakery), and also helping them make a business by selling compost fertilizer. According to Fertilizer Machinery (n.d.), organic farming drives the Philippine market for organic fertilizer by increasing demand for it. Secondly, Hu S. stated that the organic waste we produce at home can be recycled quite effectively through composting. More than 28% of what we discard is made up of food scraps and yard trash. In addition to having a negative impact on the environment, processing food waste is also expensive. Lastly, according to Douglas J. eggshells contain a lot of calcium. The growth and development of plants depend on this macronutrient. Plants can develop more quickly because the calcium in eggshells speeds up the process of constructing cell walls. Additionally, it can aid in reducing the danger of blossom end rot. This shows that our partner institution can make use and benefit from composting and from our eggshell compost project by helping them reduce their waste (from their bakery), and also help them make a business by selling compost fertilizer because first, the demand for compost is high, thus giving this business the potential to be a success, and secondly, this eggshell compost provides nutrients to the plant thus can make the eggshell compost effective. Lastly, this can help in reducing their waste (from their bakery) because composting can be made with our own food wastes.

Problem

- Is there a significant difference in the height of the plants that are exposed to eggshells and those that are not?

Hypothesis

- If we add eggshells to compost, then the plant will grow higher.
